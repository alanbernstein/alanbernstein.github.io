also the idea of "modeling luck" in terms of exposure to new activities, and how to maximize luck (and/or utility, when looking for novel experience in life) each activity provides some amount of diminishing returns, a sort of hump-y function of time, and "total experience" or "total luck exposure" is a weighted sum of these f(time_commitment) functions, where sum(time_commitment) <= total_free_time

my conversational ability is kind of like a LTI transfer function (except mine is not actually linear)

  • its locally time-invariant, that is, slowly-changing characteristics over time.

    • characteristics also depend on the person im talking to, or more simply, how comfortable i am talking with them

  • its causal - it works only in response to input

  • its BIBO stable, and more than that, responses have finite energy - they all die away eventually. for some inputs (in the MIMO sense), response is just 0. maybe UIBO? - unbounded input, bounded output

  • linearity: response depends on instantaneous "features" of input, which is a concept not captured by SISO LTI.

    • if MIMO, then each input/output can be thought of as a "conversation topic", ie, politics, science, philosophy, popular media, etc

    • the MIMO transfer function matrix is probably nearly diagonalâ€¦ how exactly does it deviate from diagonal? that is, how do the "features" of an input signal (cross terms) affect the output?

    • can think of each i/o as having a positive and negative direction - positive matches my views, negative opposes my views

    • MY response is decidedly not homogeneous - eg, strong response to positive political input, but zero response to negative political input. this represents the fact that i am "reserved" in conversation

      • a lot of people probably have saturation-like effects on their output - a limit to how much they have to say about a given topic.

      • some people probably have saturation-like effects on their input too - a limit to how much they want to hear about a topic.

    • superposition probably holds

    • not characterizable by impulse function because nonlinear

  • conversation between two people can be represented by feedback

  • conversation including M topics between N people requires ~M*N^2 2-way links - one for each topic between each pair of people

Conversational interaction resembles a system in the "LTI system" sense, though not necessarily linear nor time-invariant. Think of an individual as maintaining a transfer function, with heard speech as input, and spoken speech as output. Proper LTI systems and transfer functions are categorized with a variety of properties, many of which fit this analogy well.
